The 18 Shakthi Peethas are the holy places where people worship Goddess Parvathi in various Avatars. According to Hindu Mythology, there was a king named Daksha. He has 28 daughters, out of those 27 were married to chandra(the Hindu god of the moon). The eldest daughter was Sati, fell in love with Lord Shiva. Without listening to her father's words Sati married Lord Shiva and went to kailasa. After a long time, Daksha was conducting a yagna, he invited almost all gods and maharshis except Shiva and Sati. ... Knowing this Sati said she don't need any Invitation to her father's place and left without Shiva as he said he will not come. At the yagna, Daksha insulted Sathi and Lord Shiva. Humiliated Sati sacrificed herself in the yagna fire. Knowing all this happened, Lord Shiva got angry and sent his ganas and Veerabadhra. Veerabadhra beheaded Daksha. After all gods worshiped Shiva to stop his violence, Shiva fixed Dakshas head with a goat. Out of guilt and remorse, Shiva carried Sati's body, reminiscing about their moments as a couple, and roamed around the universe with it. All the gods are worried about the condition of Shiva because it will damage the life. Therefore, Lord Vishnu chopped pieces of Sati Mata's dead body, scattering them on the earth with his Sudarshan Chakra. These pieces of Sati Mata's body are now called Shakti Peethas. The Shakti Peethas has its mention in the Kalika Purana and Ashtashakti.Out Of 101 places where the body parts of Lord Shivaâ€™s consort Sati fell, 51 shrines are important. Of these, 18 are the most important body parts. We recognize and worship them as Ashtadasha Shakti Peethas. One is in Pakistan-occupied Kashmir (the temple was destroyed), the other is in Sri Lanka and the other 16 Shakti Peethas are in our country.
